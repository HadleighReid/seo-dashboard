📚 Measurement guide ▼ Expand
Measurement cadence
Weekly 15 min
Total site clicks & impressions — trailing 28 days vs prior 28 days. Note any pages moving significantly in either direction.
⚠ Clicks dropping: check which pages declined and cross-reference with recent changes.
After each Phase 4B batch 20 min
CTR on every URL updated in that batch — 28 days before vs 28 days after change date.
⚠ CTR drops: revert or write a third version. Don't leave a failing title for more than 4 weeks.
After each refresh publish 20 min
Set a GSC annotation on publish date. Check back at 4 weeks and 8 weeks — position trend and impressions for the refreshed URL.
⚠ No position movement at 8 weeks: check indexing, verify internal links, assess content depth.
Monthly 30 min
Top 20 pages by impressions — position trends. Coverage report for new errors. Update Dashboard tab in tracker.
⚠ Coverage errors: fix immediately. Position declines after refresh: diagnose before changing anything else.
Quarterly 1–2 hrs
Export GSC data for top 50 pages. Compare clicks, impressions, CTR, position against Q3 2026 baseline. Paste into Quarterly Scorecard below.
⚠ Flat or declining clicks after 3 months: audit redirects, indexing, and internal links. Isolate bottleneck first.
Measurement watchouts
📅 Seasonality
3PL search volume spikes in Q4 and Q1. Month-over-month comparisons in these windows are unreliable. Use year-over-year comparisons for Q4/Q1 data instead.
⏳ Google index lag
A page published or refreshed today won't show full GSC data for 2–4 weeks. GSC also has up to a 3-day processing delay. Wait at least 28 days before drawing conclusions.
📈 Position averaging
GSC averages position across all queries a page ranks for. "Average position 25" may mean position 5 for the primary keyword and 80 for long-tail variations. Filter by URL then check the Queries tab to see primary keyword position separately.
⚙ Simultaneous changes
Running redirects, title fixes, and content refreshes in the same window makes it hard to isolate what caused an improvement or decline. GSC annotations are essential — add one every time a batch goes live.
🤖 AI traffic displacement
LLMs are answering some queries directly that previously drove clicks. Impressions may hold while clicks stagnate. Track AI citations manually for target queries quarterly. Being cited in AI answers is brand visibility even without a click.
